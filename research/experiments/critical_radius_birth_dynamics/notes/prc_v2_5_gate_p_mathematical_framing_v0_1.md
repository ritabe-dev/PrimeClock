# PRC v2.5 Gate P Mathematical Framing

Status: Gate P framing aid.  This note does not authorize a public release.

## Position

The v2.5 candidate is best read as a finite exact signed containment
certificate for checked finite scopes, not a public theorem and not a general
predictor.  The complete separation observed in the checked rows is a checked
finite certificate: it records that positive signed containment margin exactly
separates close lifts from capacity non-close controls in the recorded
B4-to-B5, B5-to-B6, and B6-to-B7 transition scopes.

## Why It Is Not A Predictor

The phase margin uses the terminal new-prime arc and the pre-final residual
component.  It is therefore close to an exact certificate for closure, not an
independent prefix-only prediction feature.  Prefix lineage, grammar, width, and
capacity remain useful diagnostics, but v2.5 does not claim that they explain or
predict future births generally.

## What The Checked Scopes Mean

The checked finite scopes show that capacity alone leaves many false positives,
while the signed containment certificate has zero non-close positive-margin rows
in the historical B5/B6/B7 birth transition scopes.  These scopes support a
finite computational claim only.  They do not establish an asymptotic law, a
layer-general theorem, or B8 coverage.

## B8 Boundary

B8 is a selected stress-control sample only.  The selected 32 close rows, 576
sibling non-birth controls, 64 matched non-birth controls, and k8 sample overlap
of 1 are stress-control diagnostics.  They are not coverage, recall, holdout
validation, a B8 public theorem, or proof that the separator generalizes.

## Exact-Hull Role

The exact-hull obstruction is supporting structure.  In the checked scopes,
multi-component parent residual sets are hull-obstructed and every checked close
row has a single-gap precursor.  This supports the finite-certificate story but
does not by itself prove a public theorem for all layers.

## Gate P Reading

Gate P should treat v2.5 as public-framing review material for a finite exact
certificate.  The public theorem decision remains not ready until public title,
abstract, claim wording, non-claim wording, release metadata, and DOI readiness
are reviewed together.
